üìä Preflop Ranges & Strategy

Position-Based Opening Ranges (9-handed Live)

UTG / UTG+1 (Tightest)

Raise: AA-77, AKs-ATs, KQs, AKo-AQo

~10-12% of hands. Be tight - 8 players behind you.

Middle Position (MP / LJ)

Raise: AA-55, AKs-A9s, KQs-KTs, QJs-QTs, JTs, AKo-ATo, KQo

~15-18% of hands. Add suited connectors, more broadways.

Hijack (HJ)

Raise: AA-22, AKs-A2s, KQs-K9s, QJs-Q9s, JTs-J9s, T9s-98s, AKo-A9o, KQo-KJo

~22-25% of hands. Getting wider, but still selective.

Cutoff (CO)

Raise: All pairs, all suited aces, suited kings to K5s, suited connectors/gappers 54s+, suited broadways, offsuit broadways ATo+, KJo+

~28-32% of hands. Very profitable position.

Button (BTN) - Most Profitable Position

Raise: All pairs, all suited aces, all suited kings, most suited queens, all suited connectors, most offsuit broadways

~40-50% of hands. You always act last postflop!

Small Blind (SB)

vs Limpers: Raise strong hands to isolate, complete with suited connectors

vs Raise: 3-bet or fold most hands, calling is usually bad OOP

Big Blind (BB)

Defense: You get great odds. Defend wider vs late position, tighter vs early position.

Live Poker Adjustments

üí° Open Larger: Standard online is 2.5-3x. Live, use 4-5x + 1BB per limper. Players call more.
üí° Isolate Limpers: When players limp, raise to 4-5x + 1 per limper. Attack their weak ranges.
üí° Respect 3-Bets: Live 3-bets usually mean QQ+, AK. Fold hands like JJ, AQo to tight 3-bettors.
üí° Table Dynamics: If table is super loose, tighten up. If tight, steal more blinds.

3-Betting Strategy

Value 3-Bets (vs Any Position)

AA, KK, QQ, AKs, AKo - always 3-bet for value

JJ, AQs - 3-bet vs late position opens, flat vs early position

Bluff 3-Bets (Primarily from BTN/SB)

A5s-A2s: Blocker to AA, good playability, can make nuts

76s, 65s, 54s: Make straights, hard to put you on these

K9s, Q9s: Suited high cards with some equity

‚ö†Ô∏è Bluff 3-bets are highly recommended when playing deep stacks (200-300BB+). At 100BB vs recreationals, you often don't need to bluff 3-bet - pots are already inflated with larger open sizes and multiway action. Just wait for value hands.

3-Bet Sizing

In Position: 3x the open raise

Out of Position: 3.5-4x the open raise

Live: Go even bigger - 4-5x works well

Multiway Pots (3+ Players)

‚ö†Ô∏è C-bet MUCH less in multiway pots - especially out of position!

Out of Position (OOP):

  • C-bet very rarely - you're at a huge disadvantage
  • When you do bet, use small sizing (25-33% pot)
  • Mostly check and let IP players make mistakes

In Position (IP):

  • Use small sizing (25-33% pot) as default
  • CAN bet medium strength hands or hands needing protection for small
  • Exploitatively use bigger sizing on boards where they have lots of calling hands
üí° Recreationals massively underbluff multiway - you can manipulate pot size however you want when IP. Bet small for thin value, check back to control pot, or bomb it when you're nutted.

Turn Strategy

When You Bet Flop and Get Called

  • Barrel for value: Strong hands that want to build a pot
  • Barrel as bluff: When turn improves your range or adds equity (scare cards, flush/straight draws)
  • Check: Medium strength hands - pot control, induce bluffs

Turn Sizing

Bluffing: Use a BIG size (75-120% pot) to condense their range. This forces out marginal hands and lets you give up on river if called.

Value: Use a custom size - the BIGGEST bet you think will get called. Against calling stations, go big. Against tighter players, size down to keep them in.

üí° Don't use the same size for value and bluffs. Bluff big to fold out, value bet what they'll call.

River Strategy

River is the simplest street - no more cards to come.

Value Betting the River

üí° Recreationals are calling stations - go for thin value constantly!
  • Use the BIGGEST size you think will get called
  • Value bet thinner than you would online - top pair is often good
  • Against stations, you can value bet hands you'd normally check
  • Adjust sizing based on opponent - some call any size, others need a "cheap" price

Bluffing the River

‚ö†Ô∏è Bluffing the river is NOT recommended vs recreationals. They overcall massively.

As a general rule: don't bluff the river unless you're experienced.

However, there ARE spots where river bluffs work:

  • Many turn calling hands (pair + draw) missed and will now fold
  • River card shifts equity heavily in your favor (4th flush card, scary straight complete)
  • Opponent plays leads on river but checked - they're capped and weak

These spots are advanced. Until you're experienced at reading these situations, just check back your missed draws and move on.

Playing Without Initiative (You Didn't Raise Preflop)

Check-Raising

  • For value: Sets, two pair on wet boards
  • As bluff: Strong draws (flush + straight)
  • Size: 3-3.5x the bet you're raising

Donk Betting (Betting Into Raiser)

Usually not recommended, but works on boards that favor your range:

  • Low connected boards when you defended from blinds (765, 543)
  • When you flop a monster and want to build pot

Live Player Tendencies by Type

The Nit (Very Tight)

  • Only plays premium hands
  • Folds to aggression unless they have it
  • Exploit: Steal their blinds, fold to their raises

The Calling Station

  • Calls with anything - any pair, any draw, ace high
  • Rarely raises, just calls
  • Exploit: Value bet relentlessly, NEVER bluff

The Maniac

  • Bets and raises constantly
  • High aggression, plays many hands
  • Exploit: Tighten up, trap with strong hands, let them bluff off

The TAG (Tight-Aggressive)

  • Plays few hands, but aggressive when they do
  • The hardest to exploit - good player
  • Exploit: Fight for position, 3-bet light sometimes

üíª Advanced Concepts

Bet Sizing Theory

Geometric Sizing

When you want to get all-in by the river, use bets that "geometrically" build the pot.

Example: 100bb stacks, 6bb pot on flop
To get stacks in: ~60% pot on flop, ~75% pot on turn, all-in river

Board Texture Sizing

Board TypeSizeReason
Dry (K72r)25-33%Few draws, cheap bluffs work
Medium (QJ4)50-66%Some draws, need protection
Wet (JT9ss)66-80%Many draws, charge maximum
Paired (KK4)25-33%You have range advantage

Overbetting (>100% pot)

Use when:

  • Your range can have the nuts, theirs can't (they're "capped")
  • Polarized spots - you either have it or you're bluffing
  • You hold blockers to their calling range
üí° Against recreationals, overbetting works best as a BLUFF. They massively overfold to overbets - the big size scares them. For value, use a size they'll actually call.

Blockers

Cards you hold that reduce combinations of hands your opponent can have.

‚ö†Ô∏è Blockers have very low relevancy vs recreationals. Nice to think about, but don't overweight them in your decisions.

Bluffing with Blockers

  • Nut flush blocker: You have A‚ô†, board has 3 spades - opponent can't have nut flush
  • Straight blockers: You have the T on a 987 board
  • Pair blockers: You have AA on Axx board - hard for them to have top set

Calling with Blockers

  • Block their value: Holding a J on JT9 board blocks their straights
  • Unblock their bluffs: Don't hold missed draws they'd bluff with

GTO vs Exploitative Play

GTO (Game Theory Optimal)

  • Balanced strategy that can't be exploited
  • Uses mixed strategies (bet 60%, check 40%)
  • Best vs strong, unknown opponents

Exploitative

  • Deviates from balance to exploit opponent leaks
  • Vs calling station: Never bluff, value bet thin
  • Vs nit: Bluff more, fold to their aggression
  • Best vs weak, known opponents (most live games!)
üí° In live poker, exploitative play IS more profitable than GTO.

Range Advantage vs Nut Advantage

  • Range advantage: Your overall range is stronger on this board
  • Nut advantage: You can have more of the very best hands
Board: A‚ô†K‚ô•7‚ô¶
Preflop raiser has RANGE advantage (more Ax, Kx hands)
Board: 7‚ô†6‚ô•5‚ô¶

Protection & Equity Denial

Betting to prevent opponents from realizing equity with their draws.

  • Even if opponent folds worse, denying their equity can be +EV
  • More important on wet boards with many draws
  • Less important on dry boards

‚òï Live Poker Tells & Tips

Mike Caro's Fundamental Law

üí° "Strong means weak, weak means strong" - Players act opposite to their hand strength.

Physical Tells

Signs of Strength

  • Shaking hands: Adrenaline from big hand - VERY strong
  • Looking away: Disinterest is an act - they have it
  • Stacking chips: Preparing for a big pot
  • Quick call: Often a draw or medium hand (strong would raise)
  • Relaxed posture: Comfortable = confident in hand
  • Fluid movements: Not tense, has a good hand

Signs of Weakness

  • Staring at you: Trying to intimidate = weak/bluffing
  • Reaching for chips early: Trying to scare you from betting
  • Acting strong: Forceful chip movements, verbal aggression
  • Holding breath: Nervous about bluff being called
  • Talking a lot: Often trying to distract from weak hand
  • Freeze: Sudden stillness often indicates bluffing

Timing Tells

  • Snap call: Medium hand - strong would raise, weak would fold
  • Long pause then bet: Usually strong (thinking about sizing)
  • Long pause then call: Weak call, barely getting odds
  • Snap bet/raise: Often weak (hadn't thought it through)

Verbal Tells

  • "I guess I'll call": Usually has a strong hand
  • "You got it": Often strong - feigning weakness
  • Goading ("I dare you to call"): Usually strong
  • Naming a hand ("Do you have a flush?"): Usually doesn't have it themselves

Table Selection

üí° Game selection is your biggest edge in live poker!
  • Good signs: Laughing, drinking, friendly chat, limping
  • Bad signs: Young players in hoodies, headphones, silence
  • Best times: Friday/Saturday nights, holidays
  • Check the pot sizes: Big pots = action game

Seat Selection

  • Position on fish: Sit to their LEFT (you act after them)
  • Position on good regs: Sit to their RIGHT (they act after you less)
  • Change seats: Don't be afraid to move if a better seat opens

Table Image

  • If you've been caught bluffing: Value bet more, bluff less
  • If you've shown strong hands: Bluff more, they'll give respect
  • If you're new to table: Play tight, establish solid image

Etiquette

  • ‚úÖ Act in turn, don't slowroll, tip dealers
  • ‚úÖ Be friendly - don't tap the glass (berate bad players)
  • ‚úÖ Protect your cards, announce raises clearly
  • ‚ùå Don't angle shoot, don't splash the pot

üìñ Common Spots & Lines

Playing Pocket Pairs

Big Pairs (AA, KK, QQ)

  • Always raise/3-bet preflop - build the pot
  • Don't slowplay - too many bad runouts
  • Bet for value on most boards
  • Be willing to get stacks in preflop (100bb)

Medium Pairs (JJ, TT, 99)

  • Raise preflop, but be careful vs 3-bets
  • JJ vs tight 3-bet = often a fold or call (not 4-bet)
  • Play cautiously on A/K high boards
  • Comfortable stacking off with an overpair on low boards

Small Pairs (88-22)

  • Goal: Set mine (hit a set ~12% of the time)
  • Need ~15:1 implied odds to call profitably
  • Great in multiway pots with deep stacks
  • If you don't hit a set, be ready to fold
Set Mining Math:
You hit a set ~1 in 8 times
If you call $10 preflop, you need to win ~$80 when you hit
With 100bb stacks, set mining is very profitable!

Playing AK

  • Always raise/3-bet preflop
  • C-bet most flops (even when you miss)
  • When you flop top pair: Value bet 3 streets vs calling stations
  • When you miss: Can fire 1-2 barrels, give up on dangerous runouts
  • AK is a drawing hand preflop - don't fall in love unimproved

Playing Suited Connectors (87s, 76s, 65s)

  • Best in position with deep stacks
  • Great for multiway pots
  • Look to flop draws, two pairs, straights
  • Don't overplay one pair hands
  • Be willing to give up if you miss

Playing Draws

Flush Draws

  • Nut flush draws: Bet/raise aggressively (semi-bluff)
  • Non-nut flush draws: Be careful, you can lose to higher flush
  • Flush completes: Often bet small for thin value or check back

Straight Draws

  • Open-ended (8 outs): Can semi-bluff or call
  • Gutshot (4 outs): Usually need implied odds or fold
  • Double gutter (8 outs): Same as OESD, often disguised

Combo Draws (Flush + Straight)

  • 12-15+ outs = often favorite vs made hands!
  • Play VERY aggressively - raise and re-raise
  • Can often get stacks in on the flop profitably

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Going broke with TPTK on wet boards (top pair top kicker isn't that strong)
  • ‚ùå Bluffing calling stations (they will call you down)
  • ‚ùå Not value betting thin enough (live players call light!)
  • ‚ùå Playing too many hands out of position
  • ‚ùå Slowplaying strong hands (build the pot!)
  • ‚ùå Calling 3-bets with weak hands OOP
  • ‚ùå Limping in early position

  • Stop-win: Consider it if the game gets tough

Tilt Management

Types of Tilt

  • Injustice tilt: "I deserved to win that hand!"
  • Revenge tilt: Wanting to beat a specific player
  • Entitlement tilt: "I'm better than these players!"
  • Desperation tilt: Trying to win back losses fast

Signs You're Tilting

  • Playing hands you normally wouldn't
  • Calling when you know you're beat
  • Making bigger bets to "win it back"
  • Feeling angry or frustrated

How to Combat Tilt

  • Recognize it early - have self-awareness
  • Take a walk, get food, take a break
  • Focus on decisions, not results
  • Accept that bad beats are part of poker
  • Quit if you can't shake it

üåÖ Early Stage Strategy

The Golden Rule

üí° Daniel Negreanu: "You can't WIN a tournament early, but you CAN lose it. Think in terms of survival."

Deep Stack Fundamentals (100+ BB)

Early stages play similar to cash games. Key principles:

  • Be Conservative: Avoid big confrontations with marginal hands
  • Value Bet Relentlessly: Recreational players won't fold - get max value
  • Don't Bluff: Fish are calling stations, save bluffs for later
  • Set Mine: Small pairs need 15:1 implied odds
  • Play Position: Suited connectors in position for cheap flops

Nick Petrangelo's Deep Stack Tips

  • When you start 200bb+, treat it like a cash game
  • Don't run unnecessary bluffs in 400bb pots
  • Check more often on dry boards (73% on A-high in 3bet pots)
  • Be cautious with one-pair hands for huge pots

What NOT To Do Early

‚ö†Ô∏è Stack off with top pair for 200bb
‚ö†Ô∏è Force action trying to "chip up"
‚ö†Ô∏è Bluff into players who can't fold
‚ö†Ô∏è Overplay medium pocket pairs

Capitalize on Fish

Many fish bust early. To get their chips, you must:

  • Be present (don't late register in soft fields)
  • Play the right hands that make coolers go your way
  • Identify who's capable of bluffing vs. who's not
üí° Key Insight: "Starting with 30,000 chips, weak players don't value chips. Every chip you lose is still losing you actual dollars." - Jonathan Little

üìö Middle Stage Strategy

The Transition (25-50 BB)

Stacks shrink, antes kick in, dynamics change significantly.

Key Adjustments

  • Stealing Becomes Valuable: Blinds + antes add up fast
  • Open Wider from Late Position: Antes justify more steals
  • Apply Pressure to Medium Stacks: They can't fight back easily
  • 3-Bet Lighter: Against loose openers in late position
  • Defend Your Big Blind More: You get great odds at shorter depths

Daniel Negreanu's Middle Stage Tips

  • Be mindful of stack sizes, especially short stacks at your table
  • Avoid tangling with other big stacks unless you have it
  • In the "danger zone" (12-20bb): Fold or shove, rarely min-raise

üßÆ ICM Math & Calculations

What is ICM?

Independent Chip Model - Converts tournament chips to real dollar value.

üí° Chips won are NEVER worth as much as chips lost in tournaments. The risk of losing > reward of winning the same amount.

Why ICM Matters

  • Tournament chips don't equal cash game chips
  • Doubling your stack doesn't double your equity
  • Going bust = losing ALL your equity instantly
  • Pay jumps create "mini-bubbles" throughout

When ICM Matters Most

SituationICM Impact
Money BubbleHIGHEST - $0 to min-cash is infinite % jump
Final Table BubbleVery High - massive pay jumps
Final TableHigh - each elimination = big $ jump
Early/MiddleLow - play closer to chip EV

Bubble Factor

Measures how chip odds differ from dollar odds. Higher = tighter play needed.

  • Low Bubble Factor: Play close to normal
  • High Bubble Factor: Fold strong hands, avoid marginal spots
  • Extreme (Satellites): Can fold AA when short stacks exist!

ICM by Stack Size

Your StackICM Strategy
Short (5-12bb)Low bubble factor - must gamble to survive. Shove good hands.
Medium (15-30bb)HIGH bubble factor - avoid marginal spots. Survival valuable.
Big (40bb+)Apply pressure. You can bust others without risking your tournament.

Practical ICM Adjustments

From PokerCoaching: "When ICM pressure is significant, big bets become small bets, small bets become checks. You need to be higher in your range to fight back."

üì¶ Bubble Play Strategy

What is "The Bubble"?

When one or few eliminations remain until the money. Players do things on the bubble they don't do at any other stage.

Stack Geometry on the Bubble

Your StackStrategy
Short (5-10bb)MUST gamble. Your fold equity is your friend. Shove decent hands - survival by folding isn't sustainable.
Medium (15-25bb)AVOID marginal spots. Survival has huge value. Fold solid hands to big stacks.
Big (40bb+)APPLY MAXIMUM PRESSURE. Raise wide, bully medium stacks mercilessly. They can't fight back.

Exploiting the Bubble

üí° SplitSuit: "Amateur players are overly cautious on the bubble, placing huge emphasis on just getting paid. This creates prime-time for serious chip accumulation."

Signs of Scared Money

  • Players tanking every decision (online)
  • Sudden tightness from previously loose players
  • Only playing premium hands
  • Avoiding confrontation at all costs

Big Stack Bubble Abuse

  • Open wider - medium stacks can't defend
  • 3-bet light against other big stacks stealing
  • Put maximum pressure on 15-25bb stacks
  • Be willing to call short stack shoves wider

Short Stack Survival

  • Don't blind away waiting for premiums
  • Look for spots with fold equity
  • Shove over limpers with decent hands
  • Target other short stacks who might fold

Critical Truth

Short stacks should seek spots to gamble with reasonable equity

Medium stacks should avoid marginal confrontations

Big stacks should exploit both situations

‚ö†Ô∏è Hand-for-hand play: All tables complete hands before next one. Abuse this by raising more!

üèÑ PKO Tournament Strategy

How PKOs Work

Progressive Knockout: Half buy-in ‚Üí prize pool, half ‚Üí your bounty.

  • Bust someone: Get 50% of their bounty immediately
  • Other 50% adds to YOUR bounty
  • Bounties grow progressively - huge targets late!

The Golden Rule of PKOs

üí° You can ONLY win bounties when you COVER your opponent. This changes everything!

PKO Adjustments

SituationAdjustment
You cover opponentPlay WIDER - bounty adds value to calls
Opponent covers youPlay TIGHTER - you can't win their bounty
Big bounty on opponentCall much wider, even marginal hands
Big bounty on YOUExpect wider calls - bluff less, value bet more

Bounty Ratio (LearnWPT)

Compare bounties to starting stacks:

  • High ratio: Make big deviations to bust them
  • Low ratio: Play like regular tournament
  • Higher ratio = larger equity discount for calling

Stage-by-Stage PKO Strategy

Early Stage

Bounties are small. Play similar to regular MTT. Don't chase $5 bounties and bust.

Mid/Late Stage

Bounties are HUGE. Justify very loose all-ins when you cover short stacks with big bounties.

Critical PKO Mistakes

‚ö†Ô∏è Never late register PKOs - you miss bounty equity!
‚ö†Ô∏è Don't ignore that opponents call YOU wider (for your bounty)
‚ö†Ô∏è Bluff less - people call with anything for bounties

PKO Benefits

  • Lower variance: Get paid with every knockout
  • More action: Great for exploiting loose players
  • Immediate rewards: Profit before even cashing!

  1. Running bad ‚Üí lose confidence

  • Separate process from outcome: Judge decisions, not results
  • Use a variance calculator: See what's statistically possible
  • Review your play: Confirm you're playing well despite losses
  • Take breaks: Reset mentally when running bad
  • Move down stakes: No shame, protects bankroll and mental state

"This downswing is within statistical expectations."

  • Draws have HUGE equity (wraps can have 20 outs!)

  • Protect your equity, charge the draws

üíª Advanced PLO Concepts

Blockers in PLO

Blockers are HUGE in PLO because there are more card combinations.

Bluffing with Blockers

  • Nut flush blocker: You have A‚ô†, board has 3 spades - opponent can't have nut flush
  • Straight blockers: You have T9 on 876 board - blocks some nut straights
  • Set blockers: You have AA on Axx board - blocks top set

Calling with Blockers

  • Block their value hands
  • Don't block their bluffs (missed draws)

When to Bluff in PLO

‚ö†Ô∏è Bluff MUCH less than Hold'em - players call down!
  • When you block the nuts
  • Heads-up only (never multiway)
  • When draws miss and you can rep them
  • Against thinking players who can fold
  • On scary cards that hit your perceived range

3-Betting Strategy

Value 3-Bets

  • AAxx double-suited (especially with connectors)
  • High rundowns double-suited (KQJTds, QJT9ds)
  • KKXX with good side cards double-suited

Bluff 3-Bets

  • Less effective than Hold'em - people call more
  • Low rundowns double-suited (8765ds, 7654ds)
  • A-wheel hands (A234ds) for nut low straight potential

Calling 3-Bets

  • Hands that play well postflop
  • Rundowns, suited connectors
  • NOT bare AAxx without backup!

Stack Depth Considerations

Deep Stacks (150bb+)

  • Speculative hands gain value (rundowns, suited)
  • Implied odds are huge
  • Can call more preflop raises

Short Stacks (50bb or less)

  • Premium pairs gain value (AA, KK)
  • Less room to maneuver postflop
  • Commit with strong hands earlier

Multi-Way Pot Strategy

  • Tighten way up - someone has it
  • Only continue with nut draws or made hands
  • Give up marginal holdings
  • Position is even MORE important

üî• PLO Common Mistakes

Mistake #1: Playing Too Many Hands

PLO hands LOOK playable - most are trash!

  • "Any 4 cards" mentality = losing player
  • Danglers kill your hand's potential
  • Stick to connected, suited hands
  • Fold more from early position

Mistake #2: Overvaluing Aces

‚ö†Ô∏è Bare AAxx is NOT that strong in PLO!
  • AA without backup = just one pair postflop
  • Need suited cards, connectors, or another pair
  • AA72 rainbow is a WEAK hand
  • AAJTds is STRONG (nut flush + straight potential)

Mistake #3: Chasing Non-Nut Draws

  • Jack-high flush will lose to higher flush
  • Bottom-end straight will lose to nut straight
  • Small set loses to bigger set
  • If you're drawing, draw to the NUTS

Mistake #4: Not Folding Overpairs

  • AA on 876 board = just one pair
  • Any connected board makes AA almost worthless
  • Two pair is often minimum to continue
  • When someone raises, they usually have it

Mistake #5: Betting Too Small

  • Small bets let draws in too cheaply
  • PLO draws have HUGE equity
  • Bet 66-100% pot to protect
  • Give them bad odds to chase

Mistake #6: Slowplaying

  • Any turn card can make someone the nuts
  • Giving free cards is DEADLY in PLO
  • Bet your strong hands immediately
  • Build the pot while you're ahead

Mistake #7: Not Respecting Position

  • OOP in PLO is BRUTAL
  • You face tough decisions every street
  • Play much tighter from early position and blinds
  • Position advantage is magnified in PLO

Mistake #8: Calling Too Much Postflop

  • Calling with one pair = lighting money on fire
  • Calling with weak draws = same
  • Raise or fold more often
  • Be willing to let go of marginal hands
